What Merchants & Customers Actually See

Pick At Store · 2026-06-15 · read-only audit (5 agents, claims verified at file:line) · before the 141k Blinkit load

Do not let Blinkit rows reach customers until these are fixed. Five blockers — the worst is that every product with no veg flag shows a GREEN vegetarian dot, so all 141k rows (meat, fish, eggs, pet food, even electronics) would display as vegetarian to customers. Plus: products can go live at ₹0, images are hotlinked from the competitor's CDN, food ships with zero FSSAI/allergen info, and two endpoints leak the full catalog with no auth.
The single highest-leverage guard: load every Blinkit row inactive + zero-stock + with re-hosted images, surfacing to customers only after a merchant sets a real selling price and their own stock. That one rule neutralizes the ₹0-live, stale-availability, out-of-stock-flood, and broken-image risks at once.

The 5 go-live blockers

CRITICAL1. Green veg dot on everything non-veg

Verified: useProducts.ts:82 defaults isVeg ?? true. Blinkit ingestion never sets a veg flag → all 141k rows (meat/fish/eggs/pet food/cleaning/electronics) render a green vegetarian dot, and it carries into the cart. The dining path defaults the opposite way — proof this is an accident, not policy.

Fix: change the default to ?? undefined (the card already hides the dot when veg is unknown); only set the flag where Blinkit gives a reliable food tag. ⚠ useProducts.ts is @lock — needs your chat approval before I touch it.

CRITICAL2. Products can go live at ₹0

Blinkit data is MRP-only (no selling price). Both write paths coerce a missing price to 0 and force active=true (index.ts:10455 / :10526); the only check is price ≤ MRP, which ₹0 passes. The customer sees "₹0" with a working Add button.

Fix: reject price ≤ 0 && active=true at every write layer; require a selling price > 0 before a listing can go active; hide ₹0 rows on the consumer side as defense-in-depth.

CRITICAL3. Images hotlinked from the competitor's CDN

Ingestion stores the raw cdn.grofers.com URL and the consumer renders it directly with no re-hosting and no error fallback. If Blinkit rotates its CDN or blocks hotlinking, all 141k images break to grey boxes at once — plus it leaks your storefront traffic/referer to a competitor and is brand/IP exposure.

Fix: re-host every image to your own Supabase bucket at ingest (download → upload → store the PAS URL), exactly as merchant uploads already work. Add an onError placeholder on the card as a stopgap. Until this runs, the 141k load is un-shippable to customers.

CRITICAL4. Full catalog leaks with no auth

GET /products (index.ts:1087) and GET /merchants/:id/inventory (index.ts:5550) have no auth guard and return the entire Product row — including source, source_product_id (the Blinkit ID), product_url, extra_data — to any caller, under permissive CORS. After the load that's all 141k rows + their Blinkit provenance, exposed publicly.

Fix: add auth (requireAdmin / requireUser+ownership) + an explicit field whitelist (never return source/source_product_id/product_url/extra_data). Pair with DB column-grants/a sanitized view so direct-Supabase reads are covered too. (This overlaps Phase 1 / Phase 8 of the inventory plan.)

CRITICAL5. Food ships with zero mandatory info

No FSSAI licence, allergens, ingredients, net-quantity, country-of-origin, manufacturer, or expiry — none of these fields exist on the Product model and ingestion captures none. The Vertical.requiresFssai flag exists but isn't enforced on the listing path.

Fix: gate food verticals (requiresFssai=true) — suppress food products from the storefront until the legally-required declarations are captured at ingest. Confirm the exact legal threshold with counsel. (Ties to the agreement-compliance FSSAI gap.)

Also found (high / medium)

  • ~1,000-row cap (high): the merchant catalog does select('*') with no pagination → merchants can reach ~1% of 141k and search looks broken. Move search/pagination server-side.
  • Name-only dedupe hides sizes (high): "Amul Gold (500ml)" and "(1L)" collapse to one row, and pack-size isn't shown anywhere → distinct SKUs become unpickable. Key dedupe on name+pack+brand; show pack size.
  • Brand invisible on storefront (high): near-duplicate Blinkit items can't be told apart by customers; brand isn't even selected. Add it to the card.
  • uom precedence bug (medium): index.ts:2572 ternary never returns the plain uom string → pack size blank on most Blinkit items. One-line fix.
  • is_deleted not filtered (medium): consumer queries filter active only; a soft-deleted-but-active listing would still show. Add .eq('is_deleted', false).
  • Raw Blinkit subcategory as section header (medium): the storefront groups by Blinkit's own subcategory string, ignoring your curated taxonomy. Map via category_id/vertical_id (this is what Phase 2 enables).
  • Names/deeplinks unsanitized (medium): Blinkit names/brands flow verbatim; the CSV-import path even maps a blinkit.com deeplink onto the product. Strip competitor tokens + drop product_url at ingest.
  • No "Blinkit/source" indicator for merchants (medium): Blinkit rows look identical to curated PAS data; your "control what reaches merchants" goal has no UI enforcement point.

Guards to put in place before the load

  1. Re-host all images to your bucket (blocking) + onError placeholder.
  2. Load Blinkit rows inactive + zero-stock until a merchant sets price + stock (the master guard).
  3. Block ₹0 live listings at every write layer.
  4. Fix the veg default to "unknown = no dot" (needs @lock approval).
  5. Gate food categories behind FSSAI/declarations.
  6. Lock + whitelist the two leaking endpoints; column-protect source/provenance.
  7. Sanitize names + drop deeplinks; scan the source file for competitor text.
  8. Fix uom precedence + is_deleted filter + dedupe/brand/pack display.
