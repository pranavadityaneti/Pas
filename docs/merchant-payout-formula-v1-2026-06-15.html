2 · The formula + worked example

All money rounded to paise: round2(x) = Math.round(x × 100) / 100. Computed per order at weekly close, then summed for the cycle's net payout.

gross        = order.totalAmount                  // customer-paid total
itemsBase    = Σ(item.price × item.quantity)      // pre-GST, pre-coupon
rate         = CommissionRule(category, orderType, turnoverTier)   // 2–7%

commission       = round2(itemsBase × rate / 100)
gstOnCommission  = round2(commission × 0.18)       // NEW — 18%
gatewayFee       = round2(gross × 0.019)           // NEW — 1.9% of gross, all-in
tds              = round2(gross × 0.001)           // NEW — 0.1% §194-O
couponReimb      = (coupon funded by PLATFORM) ? couponSlice : 0
clawback         = refund amount (if any)

orderNet = round2( gross
                 − commission
                 − gstOnCommission
                 − gatewayFee
                 + couponReimb
                 − clawback
                 − tds )

cycleNetPayout = round2( Σ orderNet across the merchant's orders for the IST week )

4 · Build plan

Each step is its own PR — one change, tsc clean, diff shown, then STOP for approval. Step 2 is the only DB/money-path change.

Step 1 (optional, free) — capture Razorpay's real fee

At order creation, the Razorpay payment object is already fetched but its .fee / .tax / .method are discarded. Persist them into order.metadata. Zero behaviour change. Optional because v1 uses a flat 1.9%; this only matters if you later want exact per-order fees. Additive · tsc · deploys with the API.

Step 2 — settlement engine: add the three deductions (the real money path)

  • Wire gstOnCommission = round2(commission × 0.18) into net payout (reserved column already exists).
  • Add gatewayFee = round2(gross × 0.019) as a per-SALE-line column (mirrors commissionAmount) + cycle aggregate.
  • Add tds = round2(gross × 0.001) as a per-line column (new, correctly named tds_amount) + cycle aggregate.
  • DB migration for the two new columns (line + cycle). needs explicit migration approval
  • Lowers every merchant's net vs the old 0.98 placeholder → merchant heads-up before launch.
  • Verify against a dry-run cycle before it's real.

Step 3 — read-only endpoint GET /merchant/payout/today

Runs the same formula on today's COMPLETED + payment-verified orders, merchant-scoped (auth required). Returns a labelled breakdown { gross, commission, gstOnCommission, gatewayFee, couponReimb, clawback, tds, net }. No writes.

Step 4 — merchant app: kill the 0.98, call the endpoint

Replace the client-side × 0.98 in useEarnings.ts with a fetch to the new endpoint; relabel "Estimated Payout (Today)" → "Today's net (est.)". Ships via the already-pending merchant 1.2.5 native build (no separate OTA needed — the version is already bumped for the eSign build).

5 · Tax & compliance note — for the CA

This is a framework to confirm with a chartered accountant, not tax advice. Rates below changed in 2024; applicability is fact-specific and depends on PAS's settlement money-flow (still undecided — no payout vendor chosen yet).
LevyWhat it isRate (2026, to confirm)
TDS — Income Tax §194-OThe e-commerce operator withholds tax on gross sales it facilitates and pays to the seller; seller claims credit.0.1% of gross (reduced from 1% on 1 Oct 2024). Nil for resident individual/HUF sellers with gross ≤ ₹5L/FY and PAN furnished.
TCS — GST §52The operator collects tax on net taxable supplies where it collects the consideration.0.5% (0.25% CGST + 0.25% SGST; reduced from 1% on 10 Jul 2024). Not selected for v1 — flag available.
GST on commissionPAS's 18% GST tax invoice on its own commission/platform fee to the merchant; merchant claims input credit.18% on commission. Enabled in v1.

Questions for the CA:

  • Is PAS the "e-commerce operator responsible for paying the participant" under §194-O? (Depends on whether money flows through PAS or settles directly to merchants via the eventual payout vendor.)
  • Confirm the 0.1% §194-O rate and the ₹5L/PAN threshold logic for our seller mix.
  • Does GST TCS §52 (0.5%) also apply, or only §194-O?
  • Is PAS GST-registered and required to raise an 18% GST invoice on commission today?

Why it matters: if §194-O / §52 ARE mandatory and PAS does not deduct, the liability (tax + interest + penalty) falls on PAS as the operator — not the merchant. Real settlements are not running yet, so there is a clean window to confirm before any money moves.

6 · Risks & open confirmations

  • Payout drops vs the old 0.98. Real fees make the number lower than the placeholder merchants may have seen — communicate before launch.
  • Two different bases. Commission is on the items subtotal; gateway fee + TDS are on gross. The merchant statement should show each line on its own base to avoid "double-deduction" confusion.
  • Gateway fee on refunds. Razorpay typically does not refund its fee when an order is refunded, so the merchant correctly eats it — confirm this is intended.
  • Estimate vs final. "Today's net (est.)" will diverge from the final weekly net payout when late refunds, held orders, or post-day completions occur — it's labelled an estimate.
  • Confirm: is 1.9% all-in (GST-inclusive), or 1.9% + 18% GST (≈2.24%)? v1 assumes all-in 1.9%.
  • Column naming. New tds_amount column added rather than reusing the misnamed tcsAmount, to keep the tax head correct for filing.

7 · Rollout gates

  • Build now, flip later. The formula + columns can be built and merged now; the TDS/GST flags flip to "live deduct" only after the CA sign-off.
  • Operational prerequisites for any real settlement (independent of this work): assign commission categories per merchant in admin → Finance → Settlements; turn off cron dry-run; choose a payout vendor (Razorpay X is unsupported for our business type).
  • Step 4 rides the merchant 1.2.5 build, not an OTA.
