Merchant Agreements — Severe Points for CA / Legal Re-edit

2026-06-19 · Reviewed: the 3 in-app vendor agreements (Grocery 2% · Other Stores/Meat 5% · Restaurant 5%/7%) as ingested into merchant signup · Standard body 35 sections, Restaurant body 35 sections
How to read this: each point cites the exact clause (Standard body §, and the Restaurant body's different § where it differs), states the problem and the risk, and flags whether a CA or LAWYER (or both) must re-edit it. This is a flag-list for your professionals, not legal/tax advice. Severity: CRITICAL HIGH MEDIUM.

A. Financial structure & tax — re-draft before going live (CA + Lawyer)

CRITICALLAWYER + CA1. The agreement puts PAS in the flow of funds → unlicensed payment aggregation (RBI)

Std §3.5 / Rest §3.7 (Settlement): "PAS shall remit payments to the Merchant after deducting applicable commissions… taxes, government deductions…" · §23 Payment Services · §29 Chargeback ("PAS may recover… from future Merchant settlements")
Problem: the wording has PAS collecting the customer's money and settling it onward to the merchant. Collecting + settling funds on behalf of third-party merchants is payment aggregation, which under the RBI Payment Aggregator Guidelines may be done only by an RBI-authorised PA, through a bank-held escrow/nodal account.
Fix: re-draft so an RBI-authorised payment aggregator settles funds directly to the merchant and PAS only invoices its commission separately (PAS never holds/settles merchant funds) — OR PAS obtains PA authorisation + escrow. The settlement clause must describe the actual licensed fund-flow. (CA: escrow/split-settlement + reconciliation.)

CRITICALCA2. PAS's own e-commerce-operator (ECO) GST/TDS duties are missing; "government deductions" is too vague to authorise TCS/TDS

Std §3.4 / Rest §3.6 (Taxes) · §25 (Tax Responsibility — dumps ALL tax on the Merchant) · Std §3.5 / Rest §3.7 ("government deductions")
Problem: as an e-commerce operator, PAS is itself statutorily required (no turnover threshold) to register for GST as an ECO, collect TCS under CGST §52, deduct TDS under Income-Tax §194-O, and file GSTR-8. The agreement is silent on PAS's ECO duties, pushes 100% of tax onto the merchant (§25), and only vaguely references "government deductions" — which is not specific enough to lawfully authorise withholding TCS/TDS from settlements or to handle merchant consent + invoicing.
Fix: add an ECO tax clause that (a) records PAS as ECO, (b) authorises the TCS/TDS deduction with the rates + the merchant's consent, (c) allocates GST-invoicing responsibility, and (d) cross-references GSTR-8 / TCS-credit to the merchant.

HIGHCA3. Commission is charged on "gross order value" — ambiguous, likely tax-inclusive

Std §3.3 · Rest §3.3 (pickup 5%) / §3.4 (dining 7%)
Problem: "commission of X% of the gross order value" — if "gross" includes GST/packaging, PAS is taking commission on the customer's tax, inflating PAS's take and complicating the merchant's GST/ITC.
Fix: define the commission base precisely — e.g. "net order value, exclusive of GST and any delivery/packaging charges."

B. The paid fee vs the "Lifetime Access" promise — contract does not match what's charged (Lawyer + CA)

CRITICALLAWYER4. The agreement says "one-time onboarding fee"; the app charges "LIFETIME ACCESS" — with a premium tier the agreement never mentions, and PAS can still terminate at will with no refund

Std §3.1 ("one-time onboarding fee of INR 999… per branch") / Rest §3.1 (INR 2,999) — vs the in-app StepSubscription screen ("LIFETIME ACCESS", "Pay & Unlock", ₹999 / ₹2,999 × stores). Conflict with Std §18 / Rest §19 (terminable by PAS at any time).
Problem: three mismatches: (i) the contract calls it an "onboarding fee" but the product sells "Lifetime Access" — there is no "lifetime listing right" clause anywhere; (ii) the Standard agreement has no ₹2,999 "premium tier" the app charges; (iii) the agreement lets PAS suspend/terminate at will (§18.3/§19.3) with no refund — so a merchant pays for "lifetime" access PAS can revoke tomorrow. This is a deceptive/unfair-term + mis-selling exposure, and the contract doesn't actually authorise the amount charged.
Fix: add the real pricing (incl. premium tier) and an explicit "Lifetime Listing Right" definition; reconcile "lifetime" with the termination clause (what survives, what's refunded on PAS-initiated termination); see Point 5.

CRITICALLAWYER5. No refund / cancellation clause for the paid fee at all

Entire §3 (Commercial Terms) — silent on refundability
Problem: merchants pay ₹999–₹2,999+ but there is no clause covering refunds — cooling-off, merchant never goes live, or PAS terminates the account. Combined with the "Lifetime Access" wording this is a clear consumer-protection / unfair-term risk.
Fix: add an explicit refund & cancellation policy (e.g. cooling-off window; pro-rata or no-refund stated clearly; refund treatment when PAS terminates without merchant fault).

C. One-sided / potentially unenforceable terms (Lawyer)

CRITICALLAWYER6. PAS may unilaterally change commissions & commercial terms

Std §3.6 / Rest §3.8 ("PAS may revise commissions, onboarding fees, settlement schedules, or other commercial terms by providing reasonable prior notice") · §20.3 / §33.3 (revise policies)
Problem: a unilateral right to change the merchant's core economics (incl. commission) is a classic unfair contract term and may be unenforceable; the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules also constrain one-sided changes.
Fix: give the merchant a right to reject a material change and exit without penalty within a defined notice window (e.g. 30 days); "reasonable prior notice" → a fixed period.

HIGHLAWYER7. Liability cap = "3 months of PAS's commissions"; indemnity is one-sided & uncapped

Std §17.2 / Rest §18.2 (cap) · Std §17.1 / Rest §18.1 (no consequential damages) · Std §16 / Rest §17 (Merchant-only indemnity, uncapped, no PAS reciprocal)
Problem: the cap is PAS's commission (a tiny number), not the merchant's loss — likely unconscionable, and unenforceable for non-excludable liabilities (gross negligence, wilful default, statutory/DPDP data-breach liability, death/injury). The merchant indemnifies PAS broadly and without cap, while PAS gives no indemnity back (e.g. for PAS's platform/IP/data breach).
Fix: carve out non-excludable liabilities from the cap; make the indemnity mutual, add a cap + a standard indemnity procedure (notice, defence control, mitigation).

HIGHLAWYER8. Very broad, subjective immediate-termination grounds; no cure period

Std §13.5 / Rest §14.5 · Std §18.3 / Rest §19.3 · §26 · §28 — "reputational risk to PAS", "reasonable suspicion", "any circumstance that may adversely affect… the Platform"
Problem: these subjective grounds let PAS terminate immediately and swallow the ordinary notice (Rest §19.2's 30-day notice). With a paid "lifetime" fee and no refund (Points 4–5), abrupt termination on vague grounds is a strong unfairness + dispute risk.
Fix: narrow the immediate-termination grounds, add cure periods for curable breaches, and tie any termination to the refund clause.

D. Execution & enforceability (Lawyer)

CRITICALLAWYER9. Electronic-signature validity (IT Act) + stamp duty

§24 Electronic Execution ("acceptance… equivalent to a physical signature") · in-app method = a finger-drawn signature image
Problem: (a) under the IT Act 2000 a legally recognised "electronic signature" is an Aadhaar eSign or a DSC; a drawn-signature image may not meet the statutory test, weakening enforceability. (b) Stamp duty — the agreement appears unstamped; an unstamped/under-stamped commercial agreement is inadmissible as evidence under the Indian Stamp Act / Telangana stamp law.
Fix: confirm/upgrade the execution method (Aadhaar eSign / DSC, or robust audited click-wrap), and arrange e-stamping for each executed agreement.

E. Regulated goods, data & grievance compliance (Lawyer)

HIGHLAWYER10. No obligation to provide/display FSSAI & drug licences; PAS's own platform licences not addressed

Std/Rest §4.3 + §5.1 (merchant must "hold" licences) — but nothing requiring the licence number to be collected, verified, and displayed, and nothing on PAS's own FSSAI e-commerce / drug-licence obligations
Problem: the platform lists food (restaurants, grocery, meat, bakeries) and Pharmacy & Wellness (medicines). FSSAI e-commerce rules require display of each food seller's FSSAI licence + the platform's own licence; online medicine sale needs drug-licence/e-pharmacy compliance. The agreements only say the merchant must "hold" licences — not provide/display them.
Fix: add mandatory licence-submission + display + verification obligations (FSSAI no., drug licence), and a clause on PAS's own platform licences; consider restricting pharmacy until cleared.

HIGHLAWYER11. Data-protection roles undefined (DPDP Act 2023)

§22 Data Protection — references DPDP 2023 but does not define Data Fiduciary vs Processor, consent, breach-notification to the Data Protection Board, or a DPA
Problem: the platform handles customer + merchant personal & financial data, but the clause never says who is the Data Fiduciary/Processor, the consent mechanism, or breach-notification to the Board (it only says notify "PAS"). No Data Processing Agreement.
Fix: define data roles, add a DPA + consent + Board-notification obligations under DPDP 2023.

MEDIUMLAWYER12. Grievance clause uses a person's personal mobile/email; verify CPECR / IT-Rules completeness

§32 Grievance Redressal — "Grievance Officer: Krishna Onguru, Email: krishna@pickatstore.io, Contact: 7842287373"
Problem: a personal individual's mobile/email is published in every signed merchant contract (privacy/operational risk), and the clause should be checked against the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020 + IT Rules 2021 (whether a Nodal Officer is also required, and exact timelines).
Fix: use a role-based address (e.g. grievance@pickatstore.io) and confirm the full officer set + statutory timelines.

F. IP & drafting defects (Lawyer)

HIGHLAWYER13. PAS's licence to the merchant's brand/menus "survives termination"

Std §10 / Rest §11 (revocable licence to use name, logos, images, menus) — but §27 Survival lists "intellectual property rights" as surviving termination
Problem: §27 makes the IP licence survive termination — so PAS could keep using a departed merchant's brand/logos/menus indefinitely, contradicting the "revocable" licence.
Fix: the merchant IP licence should end on termination (with a short wind-down to remove listings/marketing); remove "intellectual property rights" from the merchant-side survival list.

MEDIUMLAWYER14. Structural defects — numbering jumps 33 → 35, and a duplicated "POLICY FRAMEWORK" section

Both bodies skip §34 (33 → 35). Standard body has TWO "POLICY FRAMEWORK" sections (§20 and §33) with near-identical text.
Problem: mis-numbering + duplicate sections undermine clarity and look unprofessional to any reviewer or court. (These were transcribed verbatim from the source PDFs — i.e. the defect is in the founder PDFs.)
Fix: renumber cleanly and merge the duplicate Policy Framework sections.

MEDIUMLAWYER15. Restaurant dining — alcohol & no-show handling not addressed

Rest §6 Dining Services · §7 Prohibited Products (generic) · §6.4 (no-shows "governed by Platform policies")
Problem: if any restaurant pre-orders/serves alcohol via the platform, that needs excise-licence treatment the agreement doesn't mention; dining no-show / cancellation terms are deferred entirely to unspecified "Platform policies" (consumer-protection alignment needed).
Fix: add an explicit alcohol/excise carve-out (or prohibition), and define the dining cancellation/no-show terms.
